Website Critique

Origami Academy is a popular chinese origami website. It is a forum based online platform, where origami folders can share instructions, finished works and stories. The site currently has over 15 thousand registered members and 190 thousand posts and is separated into 5 sections: works, exchange, leisure, download, service.

Work: In this section, folders can share pictures of their own creation, pictures of famous origamists’ work, and monthly contest foldings. It is a great place to make like minded friends and get feedbacks about your creation.

Exchange: This section is a place to post for help about a particular folding step or any question related to a model in general.

Leisure: This section is for discussion none related to origami. The contents are very random that vary from football matches to personality test.

Download: Download is the major section of Origami Academy. It offers a vast amount of instructions in many forms. From here you can find video tutorial, picture instructions, diagrams, crease patterns. In each segment instructions are sorted into categories of different types like, animal, vehicle, plants, characters and so on.

Service: Service section is where the managers of the site will post notice and guides.

This site particular important to me. It is where I started with my origami passion and where I get most of my instructions from. Personally I haven’t contributed to the site with my own instructions nor have I participated in the discussions, but I received lots of help, looking through others’ posts. The site is very well-built, with its purpose to serve the need of folders to communicate with each other and to get resources for folding. It has grown very mature in the past 7 years that I used this site. The section are easy to navigate and most importantly as an origami website, it is easy to find the instruction you want, due to its great classification and strict post naming rules.

The biggest problem about this site is the copyright issue. As a commonly neglected problem in China, copyright ignoration makes this site a violation to the origami artist who created the models. As a origami creator myself, I understand the time and effort put into creating a model and not to mention the effort to publishing a origami book. However, in Origami Academy, there are countless instructions from photo shoots of published origami books. Moreover, the sharing of these resources are not free. Although almost costless, people are transacting for instructions with virtual currencies in the forum and none of the profit goes to the actual author. I only used the original created content after I came to the US and realized the importance of copyright. It is definitely a issue that this site needs to improve.
